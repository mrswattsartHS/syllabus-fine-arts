Welcome to Fine Art

Mrs. Watts

Welcome to an exciting journey through the world of fine art! This course will introduce you to fundamental art concepts, techniques, and creative expression. You'll explore various media, develop your artistic skills, and learn to appreciate art from different cultures and time periods.

What You'll Learn

  • Fundamental elements of art and principles of design
  • Various drawing and painting techniques
  • Color theory and application
  • Art history and cultural contexts
  • Critical thinking and art criticism skills
  • Portfolio development and presentation

Course Information

Course Description

This fine art course provides students with a comprehensive introduction to visual arts. Students will explore various artistic media, learn fundamental techniques, and develop their creative voice through hands-on projects and theoretical study.

Course Objectives

  • Develop technical skills in drawing, painting, and mixed media
  • Understand and apply elements of art and principles of design
  • Analyze and critique artwork using proper terminology
  • Explore art history and cultural contexts
  • Build a portfolio demonstrating growth and skill development
  • Express personal ideas and emotions through artistic creation

Required Materials

  • Sketchbook (9"x12" minimum)
  • Drawing pencils (2H, HB, 2B, 4B)
  • Eraser and blending tools
  • Basic acrylic paint set
  • Brushes (various sizes)
  • Additional materials will be specified for individual projects

Daily Tasks & Expectations

Studio Expectations

  • Keep work area organized and clean
  • Handle materials and tools properly
  • Ask for help when needed
  • Work quietly and respectfully
  • Store artwork properly in designated areas

Late Work Policy

Late assignments will be penalized 10% per day late. Work more than one week late will not be accepted without prior arrangement. Extensions may be granted for legitimate reasons when requested in advance.

Academic Integrity

Expectations

All artwork submitted must be your original creation. While learning from other artists is encouraged, direct copying without attribution is not acceptable.

Acceptable Practices

  • Using reference materials and photographs
  • Drawing inspiration from other artists (with proper attribution)
  • Collaborating when specifically assigned
  • Asking for help and guidance from the instructor
  • Studying techniques and methods from various sources

Unacceptable Practices

  • Copying another student's work
  • Submitting work created by someone else
  • Unauthorized collaboration on individual assignments
  • Plagiarizing ideas without proper attribution
  • Using AI-generated content without disclosure

Consequences

Violations of academic integrity will result in a zero for the assignment and may lead to further disciplinary action according to school policy.

Extension Request

When to Request an Extension

  • Illness or family emergency
  • Technical difficulties with equipment
  • Scheduling conflicts with school events
  • Other legitimate circumstances beyond your control

How to Request an Extension

  1. Email Mrs. Watts as soon as possible
  2. Explain your situation clearly and honestly
  3. Provide any necessary documentation
  4. Suggest a reasonable new deadline
  5. Wait for approval before assuming the extension is granted

Important: Extensions are not automatically granted. Requests must be made before the due date when possible. Last-minute requests may not be approved unless for emergency situations.

Extension Guidelines

  • Maximum extension is typically one week
  • Multiple extensions for the same student may not be granted
  • Extensions may affect participation in class critiques
  • All extension requests must be documented via email

Quiz Information

Quiz Topics

Quizzes will cover material from lectures, demonstrations, and required readings. Topics may include:

  • Elements of Art vocabulary
  • Principles of Design
  • Art history and famous artists
  • Color theory concepts
  • Art techniques and media
  • Art criticism terminology

Quiz Format

  • Multiple choice questions
  • Fill-in-the-blank vocabulary
  • Short answer responses
  • Image identification
  • Matching exercises

Preparation Tips

  • Review class notes and handouts regularly
  • Use the vocabulary resources provided
  • Study with classmates
  • Ask questions during class
  • Practice identifying elements and principles in artwork

Make-up Policy

Students who miss a quiz due to excused absence must make arrangements to take the quiz within one week of returning to class. No make-up quizzes will be given for unexcused absences.
