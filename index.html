About the Course

This rigorous, fast-paced yearlong course (ART010 - Fine Art) for grades 11-12 blends art history with studio projects. Prior knowledge of art vocabulary and methods is expected. Foundations of Art or World History recommended.

Throughout this course, you'll explore various art movements, master different techniques, and create a diverse portfolio that showcases your growth as an artist. We'll journey through time, from ancient cave paintings to contemporary digital art, understanding how art reflects and shapes our world.

Grading Policy and Work Completion

Late Work Policy – Student Handbook Pg. 21

"All graded assignments – whether teacher- or electronically-scored – will be given a due date within the course and will be visible in the Class Plan and also in the PEAK dashboard. The consequences of submitting work after the due date is determined by each teacher. Teachers will post their late policy in the course and communicate and explain it to students at the beginning of the course."

Work in this course is to be submitted on or before the due date passes. End-of-semester and school closings may affect unit/lesson closures so ensure you attend/view live classes for announcements and read all email correspondence regarding due dates.

Late work will not be accepted. Unit and lesson close dates are hard dates.

Per Agora 2026 Policy:

  • All work will close permanently 2 weeks after the due date

  • Quizzes, reading checks, and tests will close at midnight on the date they are assigned

Academic Integrity & AI Use

Plagiarism Policy

All submitted work must be original. First offense = 0 and instruction on proper citations. Second offense = referral to administration.

Academic Integrity and Use of AI

All submitted work must be original and completed by the student. Plagiarism of any kind—including the use of copied text, images (in whole or in part), or ideas without proper citation—will not be tolerated. This includes AI-generated images or content. Students may not use artificial intelligence tools to complete assignments unless explicitly instructed to do so by the teacher. AI can be used for inspiration or brainstorming, but it must not replace the student's creative or academic efforts.

The first offense of plagiarism or inappropriate AI use will result in a zero and instruction on proper citation. A second offense will result in a referral to school administration.
